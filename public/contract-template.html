1. Purpose & Structure

The Consulting Firm provides professional consulting services related to the formation, management, and ongoing compliance of U.S. Limited Liability Companies ("LLCs") for international entrepreneurs and foreign nationals.

This MSA governs the general terms and conditions of the business relationship between the Parties. Specific services, deliverables, and fees are set forth in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") attached hereto and incorporated by reference.

2. Scope of Services

The Consulting Firm shall provide the services described in the applicable SOW, which may include but are not limited to:

  • LLC formation and state registration
  • EIN (Employer Identification Number) application with the IRS
  • Registered Agent services
  • Business bank account and payment processor setup assistance
  • Annual report filing and state compliance
  • Mail handling and forwarding from the U.S. business address
  • Ongoing administrative and operational support

Any services not explicitly listed in the SOW are outside the scope of this Agreement and may be subject to additional fees.

3. Client Responsibilities

The Client agrees to:

  1. Provide accurate, complete, and truthful information as required for LLC formation and ongoing services;
  2. Respond to requests for information or documentation within five (5) business days;
  3. Comply with all applicable U.S. federal, state, and local laws, as well as any laws of the Client's country of residence;
  4. Maintain valid identification documents (passport) throughout the duration of this Agreement;
  5. Notify the Consulting Firm promptly of any material changes in personal information, contact details, or business structure;
  6. Not use the LLC or any services provided for illegal, fraudulent, or unethical purposes.

Failure to meet these responsibilities may result in delays, additional fees, or termination of this Agreement at the Consulting Firm's sole discretion.

4. Communication & Business Hours

4.1 Business Hours

The Consulting Firm operates Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM Eastern Time (ET), excluding U.S. federal holidays ("Business Hours"). Client communications received outside of Business Hours will be addressed on the next business day. The Consulting Firm is not obligated to respond to any communication outside of Business Hours, and any urgency indicated by the Client outside such hours shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement.

4.2 Communication Channels

Official communications between the Parties shall be conducted via the following approved channels:

  • Email: Primary channel for all business correspondence
  • WhatsApp: For quick operational questions and updates
  • Telegram: For quick operational questions and updates

The Consulting Firm shall use reasonable efforts to respond to Client communications within two (2) business days.

4.3 Scheduled Calls

Scheduled video or phone calls are available only when strictly necessary for matters that cannot be adequately addressed via written communication. Calls must be scheduled in advance during Business Hours.

Should the Client request a call for matters that can be resolved via written communication, or request calls exceeding reasonable frequency, a fee of $197.00 per call shall apply. The Consulting Firm reserves the right to determine, at its sole discretion, whether a call is necessary.

5. Fees & Payment

5.1 Annual Service Fee

The Client shall pay the Annual Service Fee as specified in the Key Terms Summary and the applicable SOW. The fee covers all services described in the SOW for the applicable Contract Year (January 1 through December 31).

5.2 Payment Methods

Payments may be made via:

  • Credit or debit card through the Consulting Firm's secure online checkout system;
  • Bank wire transfer to the Consulting Firm's designated bank account.

Payment instructions and links will be provided by the Consulting Firm at the time of invoicing.

5.3 Payment Schedule

The payment schedule shall be as specified in the Key Terms Summary. If payment is to be made in installments, all installment dates and amounts will be detailed in the SOW.

5.4 Late Payment

If any payment is not received within fifteen (15) days of its due date, a late fee of 1.5% per month (or the maximum permitted by applicable law, whichever is less) shall accrue on the unpaid balance from the original due date until paid in full.

The Consulting Firm reserves the right to suspend all Services if payment remains outstanding for more than thirty (30) days past the due date, without such suspension constituting a breach of this Agreement. Services shall resume within five (5) business days of receipt of all outstanding payments and accrued late fees.

5.5 Refund Policy

All fees paid under this Agreement are non-refundable. No refund shall be issued under any circumstances, including but not limited to early termination, cancellation by the Client, or dissatisfaction with the Services. The Client acknowledges and agrees to this no-refund policy prior to making any payment.

5.6 Additional Fees

Services not included in the SOW, government filing fees, third-party costs, and expedited processing requests may incur additional charges. The Client will be notified in advance of any additional fees before they are incurred.

6. Third-Party Providers

The Consulting Firm may engage third-party service providers to assist in the delivery of certain Services, including but not limited to registered agent services, tax preparation, banking partners, and payment processors. The Consulting Firm shall exercise reasonable care in selecting such providers but shall not be liable for the acts, omissions, or failures of any third-party provider.

Any fees charged by third-party providers are separate from the Annual Service Fee unless explicitly stated otherwise in the SOW.

7. Mail Handling & Forwarding

The Consulting Firm shall receive, scan, and forward mail addressed to the Client's LLC at the U.S. registered business address. Mail will be processed during Business Hours and forwarded digitally via email within two (2) business days of receipt.

Physical mail forwarding, if requested, shall be subject to actual shipping costs plus a handling fee as determined by the Consulting Firm.

8. Confidentiality

Each Party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary, financial, and personal information disclosed by the other Party in connection with this Agreement ("Confidential Information"). Neither Party shall disclose, reproduce, or use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of obligations under this Agreement, except:

  1. With the prior written consent of the disclosing Party;
  2. As required by applicable law, regulation, or court order;
  3. To the extent necessary to engage third-party providers as described in Section 6.

This confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

9. Data Protection & Privacy

The Consulting Firm shall process the Client's personal data solely for the purpose of performing the Services under this Agreement. Personal documents, including but not limited to passport copies and identification documents, shall be stored securely using industry-standard encryption and access controls.

Personal data shall be retained only for the duration required to perform the Services and comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations (including but not limited to IRS record-keeping requirements).

For Clients who are residents of the European Union or the European Economic Area, the processing of personal data is based on contractual necessity under Article 6(1)(b) of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"). Such Clients have the right to request access to, correction of, or deletion of their personal data, subject to the Consulting Firm's legal retention obligations.

10. Tax Return Preparation

If tax return preparation is included in the applicable SOW, the Consulting Firm shall arrange for the preparation and filing of the LLC's annual U.S. tax return through a qualified third-party tax professional.

The Client is solely responsible for providing accurate and complete financial records in a timely manner. The Consulting Firm shall not be held liable for penalties, interest, or additional taxes resulting from inaccurate, incomplete, or late information provided by the Client.

Tax advisory services, personal tax returns, and tax planning are not included unless explicitly stated in the SOW.

11. Contract Year & Renewal

The Contract Year runs from January 1 through December 31 of each calendar year, regardless of the Effective Date of this Agreement.

This Agreement shall automatically renew on January 1 of each subsequent year for additional one-year terms under the same terms and conditions, unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal no later than November 1 of the current Contract Year.

Upon renewal, the Consulting Firm reserves the right to adjust the Annual Service Fee. The Client will be notified of any fee adjustment no later than November 15 of the current Contract Year. If the Client does not agree to the adjusted fee, the Client may decline renewal by providing written notice within fifteen (15) days of receiving the fee adjustment notification.

Clients onboarding after January 1 of a given Contract Year shall pay the full Annual Service Fee for that initial year. The fee shall not be prorated.

12. Termination

12.1 Termination by Client

The Client may terminate this Agreement by providing written notice to the Consulting Firm. Termination shall be effective thirty (30) days after receipt of such notice. No refund of fees paid shall be issued upon termination.

12.2 Termination by Consulting Firm

The Consulting Firm may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:

  1. The Client breaches any material term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of written notice;
  2. The Client fails to make payment for more than thirty (30) days past the due date;
  3. The Client engages in illegal, fraudulent, or unethical conduct;
  4. The Client violates the Client Conduct provisions set forth in Section 13.

12.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, the Consulting Firm shall cease all Services. The Client shall remain liable for all fees accrued prior to the effective date of termination. Sections 5.5 (Refund Policy), 8 (Confidentiality), 9 (Data Protection), 15 (Limitation of Liability), 16 (Force Majeure), 17 (Indemnification), and 20 (Governing Law) shall survive termination.

13. Client Conduct

The Client shall conduct all interactions with the Consulting Firm and its staff in a professional and respectful manner. The Consulting Firm reserves the right to terminate this Agreement immediately, without refund, if the Client:

  1. Engages in abusive, threatening, or harassing behavior toward the Consulting Firm or its staff;
  2. Makes repeated unreasonable demands outside the scope of this Agreement;
  3. Provides false or misleading information;
  4. Uses the LLC or related services for any illegal purpose.

14. Intellectual Property

All templates, processes, methodologies, and systems developed or used by the Consulting Firm remain the exclusive intellectual property of Tony Durante LLC. The Client is granted a limited, non-transferable license to use any deliverables provided solely for the operation of the Client's LLC.

16. Force Majeure

Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement if such failure or delay results from circumstances beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, government actions or restrictions, war, terrorism, labor disputes, power failures, internet disruptions, or failures of third-party service providers.

The affected Party shall provide prompt written notice to the other Party and shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects of the force majeure event.

17. Indemnification

The Client shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Consulting Firm, its members, managers, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or related to:

  3. Any inaccurate, incomplete, or misleading information provided by the Client;
  4. The Client's use of the LLC for any unlawful purpose;
  5. Any third-party claim arising from the Client's business activities.

18. Assignment

The Client may not assign, transfer, or delegate this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the Consulting Firm. The Consulting Firm may assign this Agreement to any successor entity or affiliate without the Client's consent.

19. Independent Contractor

The Consulting Firm is an independent contractor and nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties. Neither Party has the authority to bind the other or incur obligations on the other's behalf.

20. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

20.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Florida, United States of America, without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

20.2 Dispute Resolution

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall be resolved as follows:

  1. Mediation: The Parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith mediation administered by a mutually agreed-upon mediator in Pinellas County, Florida.
  2. Binding Arbitration: If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration in Pinellas County, Florida, under the rules of the American Arbitration Association (AAA). The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding on both Parties.
  3. Attorneys' Fees: The prevailing Party in any arbitration or legal proceeding shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing Party.
